Sports Psychologist

Madison Borough FC is seeking a motivated and passionate Sports Psychologist to support the mental wellbeing and performance of players across the club.

This is an exciting opportunity to be part of a forward-thinking football club committed to player development and wellbeing.

Job Description

Job Title: Sports Psychologist

Department: Performance & Player Support

Location: Madison Borough Training Ground

Role Purpose

To support the psychological wellbeing, confidence, resilience, and performance of players at Madison Borough FC. The role includes helping players to manage pressure and anxiety, teaching coping strategies for high-pressure situations, and supporting mental preparation for training and matches. The Sports Psychologist will also play an important role in aiding team cohesion, encouraging positive relationships within squads, and helping to create a united, supportive, high-performing environment.

Key Responsibilities
  • Provide one-to-one support sessions with players.
  • Deliver workshops on confidence, focus, and performance mindset.
  • Support players dealing with pressure, anxiety, setbacks, or injuries.
  • Teach coping strategies for high-pressure sporting situations.
  • Work alongside coaches to improve player mental resilience.
  • Support academy players with development and wellbeing.
  • Aid team cohesion and promote strong working relationships within squads.
  • Promote a positive, professional, and supportive team environment.

Person Specification

Essential Skills and Qualities
  • Knowledge of psychology or sports psychology.
  • Excellent communication and listening skills.
  • Ability to build trust and rapport with players and staff.
  • Strong empathy and people skills.
  • Interest in football and athlete development.
  • Ability to remain calm and supportive in high-pressure situations.
  • A real passion for sports psychology and player development.
  • Ability to communicate clearly with a range of ages and ability levels.
  • Enhanced DBS check or willingness to obtain one.
Desirable Skills and Qualities
  • Previous experience working with athletes or sports teams.
  • Previous exposure to high-performance environments.
  • Understanding of mental performance techniques.
  • Ability to deliver workshops or group sessions.
  • CPD certificates in specialist or niche areas of sport psychology.
  • Academic or research experience in newer psychological techniques and methods.
  • Awareness of current research and evidence-based approaches in sports psychology.
